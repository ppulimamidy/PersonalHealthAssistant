# USER JOURNEY 1: THE WORRIED PATIENT
### "Sarah discovers she's been managing her health in the dark"

**Persona:** Sarah Chen, 52. Project manager. Three chronic conditions, three medications, a doctor she sees every six months. She tracks nothing. She feels constantly tired but assumes it's just "getting older."

**The inciting moment:** Sarah's quarterly blood work comes back. Her doctor's office calls and says her A1C has crept from 6.2% to 6.5%. She's told to "watch her diet." She has no idea what that means or where to start.

---

### ACT 1 — The Overwhelm (What life looks like before)

Sarah opens a notebook app on her phone and searches "what to eat for prediabetes." 47 tabs later, nothing connects. She's tracking nothing. Every appointment starts from scratch. Her doctor asks: *"How have you been feeling?"* — and her honest answer is *"I don't really know."*

---

### ACT 2 — The Setup (Demo: Onboarding, 2 minutes)

She creates an account. The onboarding asks her:
- Current conditions (she types: prediabetes, hypertension, hypothyroidism)
- Current medications (Metformin, Lisinopril, Levothyroxine)
- Primary goals (manage blood sugar, more energy, less stress)

**What the platform immediately does:**
- Flags that Levothyroxine and certain supplements interact
- Pre-configures her dashboard for metabolic health tracking
- Sets up care plan targets based on clinical guidelines for her conditions
- Populates the AI context so every insight is personalized to her situation

**Demo step:** Log in as Sarah → show the Today page
- Health score ring (Overall: 68, Sleep: 71, Activity: 42, Readiness: 75)
- Three active care plan targets with current values vs. goals
- Smart reminder: "You haven't logged today's Metformin"

---

### ACT 3 — The Discovery (Demo: AI Insights, 3 minutes)

After two weeks of logging, Sarah checks the Insights tab.

The AI has spotted something her doctor never mentioned:

> **"Your symptom severity spikes by 2.3 points every Sunday evening and Monday morning. Cross-referencing with your meal logs: high-sodium takeout Sunday nights is correlated with elevated blood pressure Monday mornings, which correlates with reported fatigue and concentration issues through midday. Reducing sodium on Sundays may meaningfully improve your Monday productivity."**

She also sees:
- Her sleep is averaging 5h 40min — well below the 7-hour target for insulin sensitivity
- Her HRV (heart rate variability) has dropped 18% over the past 30 days
- Her Metformin adherence is 71% — she keeps missing the evening dose

**The moment:** Sarah realizes she's been treating her conditions in isolation. The app shows her they're all connected — the poor sleep worsens insulin resistance, the insulin resistance drives anxiety, the anxiety disrupts sleep.

**Demo step:** Click "Correlated Insights" → show the multi-system pattern card:
> "Sleep < 6h → next-day glucose runs 15-20 mg/dL higher · 87% confidence across your 30-day dataset"

---

### ACT 4 — The Win (Demo: Doctor Prep Report, 2 minutes)

Sarah's appointment is in two days. Instead of walking in with nothing, she generates a Doctor Prep Report.

The AI assembles:
- 90-day symptom timeline with severity trends
- Medication adherence by drug (Metformin 71%, Lisinopril 94%, Levothyroxine 88%)
- Three abnormal lab values worth discussing
- A narrative summary: *"Patient has identified sleep quality as a primary driver of glucose variability. Recommends discussing CGM trial and sleep intervention."*

She emails the PDF to her doctor the night before.

**At the appointment**, her doctor says: *"This is the most useful thing a patient has ever brought me."*

**Demo step:** Click Doctor Prep → Generate Report → show the PDF output

---

### ACT 5 — The Transformation (Demo: 30-Day Follow-up)

Six weeks later, Sarah's follow-up insights appear on her Today page:

> 💡 **3 findings from ~30 days ago — see what changed**

She clicks through:
- Sleep: 5h 40min → 6h 55min (+75min) ↑ Better
- Symptom severity: 6.2/10 → 4.1/10 ↓ Better
- Medication adherence: 71% → 89% ↑ Better

Her next A1C: 6.1%. Back below the prediabetes progression threshold.

**The line:** *"She didn't change because of willpower. She changed because for the first time, she could see what was actually happening."*

# USER JOURNEY 2: THE WAKE-UP CALL
### "James, 44, the guy who thinks he's healthy"

**Persona:** James Okafor, 44. Marketing director. Exercises twice a week. Drinks on weekends. Considers himself "basically fine." Has never tracked his health in his life.

**The inciting moment:** During a routine physical, his doctor says: *"Your LDL is 178, your fasting glucose is 108, and your blood pressure is 142/91. We need to talk."* He walks out with three new prescriptions and a profound sense of confusion.

---

### The Journey

James downloads Health Assistant that same night, mostly out of fear.

**Day 1:** He enters his lab results. The app immediately flags:
- LDL 178 is above the 130 threshold for his age/risk profile
- Fasting glucose 108 is in the prediabetes range
- BP 142/91 is Stage 1 hypertension

But instead of just red flags and panic, the app does something unexpected — it gives him context:
> *"These three findings are commonly linked. Insulin resistance (suggested by your fasting glucose) promotes both LDL elevation and hypertension. Addressing the root cause — metabolic health — may improve all three."*

For the first time, the test results make sense as a story, not three separate problems.

**Week 2:** The AI Agents tab. James opens the Cardiologist agent.

> **James:** "Should I be worried about these numbers?"
> **AI Cardiologist:** "At 44, with your current LDL and blood pressure, your 10-year cardiovascular risk is approximately 9%. That's in the borderline range. The encouraging news: these are very reversible at your age. Let me show you what moves the needle most..."

**Week 6:** James's glucose drops from 108 to 99 (normal range). His doctor calls, surprised.

**Demo step:** Show the Lab Results tab → enter an LDL value → watch the insight fire → show the AI agent conversation

**Future roadmap moment:**
> *"Imagine James also has a CGM patch. We see real-time glucose spikes after every Friday happy hour. The app texts him Saturday morning: 'Your glucose spiked to 167 last night, 47% higher than your baseline. Here's what the research says about alcohol and insulin resistance.' Not judgment — just signal."*

# USER JOURNEY 3: THE DOCTOR
### "Dr. Martinez gets her Saturday morning back"

**Persona:** Dr. Jennifer Martinez, MD — Endocrinologist. Panel of 380 patients. Spends 40% of each appointment re-reading the last visit's notes because patients can't remember what happened six months ago. Constantly anxious about which patients are silently deteriorating between visits.

**The problem:** The healthcare system is reactive. Patients get worse before they get seen.

---

### The Journey

Dr. Martinez's patients begin sharing their Health Assistant profiles with her practice.

**The Patients page:** She logs in and sees her roster. Eight patients are flagged this morning.

She clicks the red alert banner:
> ⚠ **3 patients have out-of-range metrics · 1 critical**

She expands it:
- **Sarah Chen** — Medication adherence dropped to 58% (Metformin). Current glucose trending up.
- **Robert Kim, 67** — LDL jumped from 142 to 189 in last 14-day labs. Critical.
- **Maria Santos, 61** — Symptom severity averaging 7.8/10 this week, up from 4.2 last week.

Before she even gets to the clinic, she's messaged Robert about his LDL and flagged Maria for an urgent call.

**During appointments:** She clicks Sarah's name. Sarah's entire 90-day health picture loads instantly:
- Adherence calendar: missed evening Metformin 14 days in a row
- Sleep trending down: 7h → 5.5h
- Glucose values available from her logged check-ins
- Active care plan showing: target A1C 6.0%, current tracking 6.4%

Dr. Martinez uses this to focus the appointment. Instead of 10 minutes establishing baseline, she has 10 extra minutes to actually help.

**The care plan feature:** Right in the provider view, she creates a care plan for Sarah:
- Title: "Improve Metformin Adherence"
- Metric type: medication_adherence
- Target: 90%
- Notes: "Set morning alarm. Take with breakfast."

This plan appears on Sarah's Today page the next time she opens the app.

**Demo step:** Patients page → show 2-column layout → click alert → load patient → show pre-appointment summary → suggest care plan

**The line:** *"Dr. Martinez used to walk into each appointment blind. Now she walks in knowing exactly what matters. She's not a better doctor — she has better tools."*

# USER JOURNEY 4: THE NUTRITIONIST
### "Maya helps her clients for the other 167 hours a week"

**Persona:** Maya Patel, RDN — Registered Dietitian. Works with 35 metabolic health clients. Her biggest challenge: she sees clients for 1 hour a week. The other 167 hours, she has no idea what they're eating, how they're feeling, or whether any of her advice is landing.

---

### The Journey

Maya asks her clients to share their Health Assistant profiles with her.

**Monday morning:** She checks her patient list. Immediately she sees:
- Client 1 has logged every meal for 14 days straight — great data
- Client 2's average daily fiber is 12g (target: 30g) — needs attention
- Client 3's symptom severity spikes every Thursday — she cross-references with Thursday meal logs and sees a pattern around high-carb lunches

**The Nutrition tab:** She can see exactly what her clients are eating. The AI food recognition means clients just photograph their food — no manual logging burden.

**The insight she couldn't have found before:**
> *"Sarah's glucose check-ins run 18% higher on days she eats breakfast alone vs. days she eats with others. Social eating appears to correlate with slower, more mindful eating. Consider this when designing meal plans."*

**Creating a nutrition care plan:**
Maya creates a plan in Sarah's profile:
- Goal: Increase daily fiber to 30g
- Metric: dietary_fiber
- Target value: 30g
- Target date: 30 days out
- Notes: "Focus on legumes and vegetables. Avoid fiber supplements."

Sarah sees this on her Today page. Progress bar updates daily.

**Demo step:** Show Nutrition view with food log → show care plan creation → show the progress bar on Sarah's Today page

# USER JOURNEY 5: THE CAREGIVER
### "David Chen manages his mother from 2,000 miles away"

**Persona:** David Chen, 28. Sarah's son. Lives in New York. His mother is in San Francisco managing three chronic conditions. He worries constantly but doesn't know how she's actually doing.

---

### The Journey

Sarah creates a share link in Settings → Care Team Sharing. She selects:
- ✅ Profile & Conditions
- ✅ Medications
- ✅ Lab Results
- ✅ Symptoms
- ✅ AI Insights

She sends David the link. He doesn't need an account — it opens in his browser.

He sees his mother's real health picture for the first time: her medication adherence, her symptom trends, her latest labs. Not alarming — reassuring. She's doing better than he imagined.

**He creates an account and saves her to his roster.** Now he gets his own Today page with a combined view of anyone he's supporting.

**The alert moment:** One Tuesday morning, David gets an alert:
> ⚠ *"Sarah Chen — Medication adherence has dropped to 48% over the past 7 days."*

He calls her. She'd been traveling and forgot her pill organizer. He helped her set up a new reminder system.

**Without Health Assistant:** He would have found out at her next doctor's appointment — three months later, after her A1C had climbed.

# USER JOURNEY 6: THE ATHLETE / QUANTIFIED SELF
### "Marcus optimizes performance, not just survival"

**Persona:** Marcus Webb, 31. Competitive amateur cyclist. Sleeps with an Oura Ring. Obsesses over recovery scores. Wants to understand exactly what drives his best performances — and what derails them.

**The setup:** Marcus connects his Oura Ring. Three weeks of baseline data flows in.

**The insight he didn't expect:**
> *"Your readiness score correlates at 0.83 with prior-night HRV. But HRV itself has a stronger correlation with alcohol consumption 48 hours prior than with training load. A single drink reduces your readiness score by an average of 7 points the next day."*

He already knew alcohol affected sleep. He didn't know the effect lasted 48 hours.

**The simulation:**
He opens the Health Twin and runs a scenario:
- "What if I eliminate alcohol entirely for 30 days?"
- Projected readiness improvement: +12%
- Projected average HRV: 68ms → 79ms

He commits to the experiment. Thirty days later, the follow-up insight confirms it.

**Demo step:** Timeline view → show Oura sleep + HRV data → Insights → correlation card → Health Twin simulation
